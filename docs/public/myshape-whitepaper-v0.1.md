**MyShape Protocol**

## Abstract

All existing identity systems — passwords, biometrics, wallets, KYC — prove that an account belongs to someone. They prove nothing about whether that someone is actually present, right now, performing the operation in question.

As AI systems achieve indistinguishability from humans across every static modality, this distinction becomes existential. A bot can create ten thousand accounts in minutes. An AI can forge a face, a voice, a fingerprint. The asymmetry is fatal.

MyShape introduces the **Presence Protocol**: a cryptographic framework that verifies human presence — not identity, not credentials, not biometrics. The protocol generates an unpredictable motion challenge, captures the user's real-time 3D kinematic response across multiple sensors, extracts a mathematical signature from the irreducible physics of their body, and produces a zero-knowledge proof that can be verified anywhere without revealing the underlying biometric data.

The protocol's security model is economic, not absolutist. It does not claim motion is unforgeable. It demonstrates that the cost of forging presence can be engineered to exceed the value of the operation being protected — and that this asymmetry holds even under the most pessimistic assumptions about AI progress.

This whitepaper presents the complete architecture: the threat model, the challenge-response protocol, the motion signature mathematics, the multi-factor verification framework, and the roadmap from Proof of Intent MVP to a full Presence Layer for the decentralized internet.

## 1. The Identity Crisis

### 1.1 What All Identity Systems Actually Prove

Every identity system in use today — from email passwords to biometric locks to hardware wallets — answers a single question:

> "Does the credential presented match the credential on file?"

It does not answer:

> "Is the human who enrolled that credential the same human presenting it right now?"

This gap has existed for decades. AI makes it fatal.

### 1.2 The AI Asymmetry

| Capability | AI (2026) | AI (2028, projected) |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Generate convincing text | ✅ | ✅ |
| Clone a voice from 3 seconds of audio | ✅ | ✅ |
| Generate a real-time deepfake face | ✅ | ✅ |
| Create 10,000 unique social media accounts | ✅ | ✅ |
| Pass a static face recognition check | ✅ | ✅ |
| Synthesize a fingerprint from a photograph | Emerging | ✅ |
| **Perform a real-time, full-body, multi-sensor coordinated motion in response to an unpredictable challenge** | **No** | **No (see §5)** |

The last row is the entire thesis of MyShape. Motion is not a file. Motion is not a template. Motion is a continuous, high-dimensional, real-time field generated by the irreducible physics and biology of a living human body.

### 1.3 Presence vs. Identity

There is a critical distinction that most projects miss:

| | Proof of Human (World) | Proof of Presence (MyShape) |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Proves** | "You are a member of the human set" | "You — this specific entity — are present right now" |
| **Model** | One-to-many membership test | One-to-one presence verification |
| **Use case** | Universal Basic Income, Sybil resistance | High-value transaction authorization, agent delegation |
| **Security requirement** | Statistical (some false accepts tolerable) | Cryptographic (false accepts catastrophic) |

MyShape is not competing with Proof of Human protocols. It operates at a different layer — the layer where a single false accept could mean a $10M unauthorized transfer.

$$\text{Cost of Forgery} \gg \text{Value of Operation}$$

## 3. The Presence Challenge Protocol

### 3.1 Why Active Challenge-Response

Passive biometric recognition — observing someone's natural movement and identifying them — is fundamentally vulnerable to AI forgery. The attacker can pre-compute, optimize offline, and replay.

Active challenge-response inverts this dynamic: the verifier issues an unpredictable motion instruction, and the prover must execute it within a strict time window. The attacker faces three simultaneous constraints:

1. **Real-time constraint**: Response must begin within ~100ms of challenge display — below current and projected AI motion generation latency
2. **Unpredictability constraint**: Challenge space exceeds 10²⁸ possible combinations — pre-computation infeasible
3. **Multi-modal constraint**: Response is captured by camera + inertial sensors simultaneously — screen-based forgery cannot satisfy both

### 3.2 Challenge Structure

A challenge is a tuple:

$$c = (\text{actions}, \text{parameters}, \text{constraints}, \text{time\_window}, \text{nonce})$$

Every challenge requires:
- **2–5 coordinated actions** across multiple joint groups
- **At least one coupling constraint** — sub-actions sharing a kinetic chain, preventing independent generation and fusion
- **Non-periodic trajectory** — irregular motion patterns that resist AI synthesis
- **Cryptographic nonce** — binding the challenge to a single session

**Example challenge**: "With your right hand, draw a counterclockwise circle. Simultaneously, lean your torso 10° to the right while maintaining a forward head gaze. Complete at moderate speed within 4 seconds."

The torso lean and right arm motion share the spinal kinetic chain. They cannot be independently generated and linearly blended without producing detectable kinematic inconsistency.

### 3.3 Challenge Entropy

The challenge space is combinatorially vast: action selection (~50 base actions), parameterization (continuous ranges for direction, amplitude, speed, duration), constraint selection, and random perturbations. The resulting entropy exceeds 10²⁸ possible challenges — far beyond enumeration.

### 3.4 Sensor Requirements

Every verification requires:
- **Camera**: 30–60 fps RGB, used for 3D pose estimation
- **Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU)**: 100–200 Hz accelerometer + gyroscope
- **Cross-sensor consistency**: Camera-derived acceleration must match IMU-measured acceleration

This dual-sensor requirement is the protocol's strongest defense against screen-based forgery. A display can deceive a camera. It cannot simultaneously deceive an IMU — the IMU measures the device's physical motion, not what's shown on screen.

## 4. The Motion Signature

### 4.1 From Motion to Mathematics

The Motion Signature is a function $\text{MS}: \mathcal{M} \to \mathbb{R}^{256}$ that maps a raw motion sequence to a 256-dimensional vector in a space where:

- **Intra-person distances are small**: The same person's signatures cluster tightly
- **Inter-person distances are large**: Different people's signatures are well-separated
- **AI-generated motion falls in between**: AI can approach but not reach the human cluster

### 4.2 Seven Dimensions of Human Motion

The signature decomposes motion into seven complementary feature groups. Each captures a different aspect of human movement — and a different failure mode of AI generation:

| Dimension | What It Measures | Why AI Struggles |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Skeletal Kinematics** | 3D bone lengths, joint angles, limb ratios | 2D→3D depth ambiguity makes true skeletal proportions underdetermined from video alone |
| **Acceleration Profile** | Statistical distribution of movement acceleration | AI acceleration tends toward excessive regularity or unstructured noise |
| **Jerk Dynamics** | Rate of change of acceleration (third derivative of position) | AI jerk is either over-smoothed or spectrally white — human jerk exhibits specific multi-scale structure |
| **Jerk Spectrum** | Frequency-domain structure of jerk (1–15 Hz) | Human jerk follows approximate 1/f scaling from multi-scale biological control; AI cannot replicate this spectral fingerprint |
| **Physiological Tremor** | 8–12 Hz micro-oscillations from the nervous system | Tremor is a byproduct of the neural control loop, not a feature of the motion trajectory — absent from AI generation |
| **Multi-Joint Coordination** | Cross-correlation structure between joint movements | AI generates locally correct but globally incoherent coordination patterns |
| **Sensor Consistency** | Agreement between camera-derived and IMU-measured acceleration | Screen forgery fundamentally cannot satisfy both — this is a physical constraint, not an algorithmic one |

### 4.3 Verification

Given an enrolled signature $\mathbf{z}_{\text{enroll}}$ and a challenge-response signature $\mathbf{z}_{\text{response}}$:

$$\text{score} = 1 - \frac{\|\mathbf{z}_{\text{enroll}} - \mathbf{z}_{\text{response}}\|}{\|\mathbf{z}_{\text{enroll}}\| + \|\mathbf{z}_{\text{response}}\|}$$

The score ranges from 0 (completely different) to 1 (identical). Verification thresholds are calibrated per risk tier.

### 4.4 Privacy

All motion processing occurs on-device. Raw video, 3D keypoints, IMU streams, and feature vectors never leave the device. Only a zero-knowledge proof and an entropy score are transmitted. The verifier learns "presence confirmed" and nothing else.

## 5. Security Analysis

### 5.1 Why AI Cannot Currently Forge Presence

The seven signature dimensions are not equally difficult for AI. The spectral dimensions — jerk spectrum and physiological tremor — exploit structural properties of AI models that are not temporary limitations but consequences of how these models work:

- **Smoothness bias**: AI motion models use L2 loss functions that penalize high-frequency content. Human jerk and tremor live in precisely the frequency bands that L2 loss suppresses.
- **Mode-seeking behavior**: Probabilistic generative models produce the "average" of the training distribution. Individual physiological signatures are outliers by construction.
- **Absence of biological substrate**: Tremor originates in the stretch reflex loop (muscle spindle → spinal cord → muscle), a physical control system AI models do not possess.

### 5.2 Multi-Factor Defense in Depth

The protocol layers five verification factors:

| Factor | Type | What It Defends Against |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Motion Signature** | Biological | AI forgery of human movement |
| **Device Attestation** | Hardware | Sensor injection, device compromise |
| **Context Graph** | Behavioral | Anomalous usage patterns, location spoofing |
| **Temporal Continuity** | Temporal | Gradual approximation attacks across sessions |
| **CoPresence** | Network | Isolated forgery without witnesses |

Factors are physically coupled — compromising one increases the difficulty of compromising others. The security margin grows superlinearly with factor count: breaking two factors costs more than twice breaking one.

### 5.3 Risk-Adaptive Security

The protocol adapts its security posture to the value at stake:

| Operation Value | Required Factors | Verification Depth |
|:---|:---|:---|
| <$1K | Motion + Device (basic) | Standard challenge, relaxed thresholds |
| $1K–$100K | Motion + Device + Context | Higher complexity challenge, tighter thresholds |
| $100K–$1M | Motion + Device + Context + Temporal | Maximum complexity, drift detection active |
| >$1M | All 5 factors + time-delayed execution | Multi-session verification + co-presence |

This tiered approach means the protocol does not impose $1M-level security on a $10 transaction — and does not allow $1M transactions with $10-level security.

### 5.4 Future-Proofing

The protocol is designed for a world where AI capabilities continue to advance:

- **Challenge unpredictability** prevents pre-computation regardless of model quality
- **Multi-sensor requirement** provides a physical floor that no software advance can breach
- **Multi-factor architecture** allows additional factors to be deployed as needed
- **Temporal drift detection** catches attackers who attempt gradual improvement across sessions

The protocol's security is not a fixed point. It is a tunable function of risk tolerance, AI capability, and available factors.

## 6. Zero-Knowledge Architecture

### 6.1 What ZK-Presence Proves

A ZK-Presence proof establishes the following statement without revealing the underlying data:

> "There exists a motion sequence, captured by an attested device in response to a specific challenge, whose Motion Signature matches the enrolled signature within threshold, and which carries sufficient biological entropy to confirm human presence."

### 6.2 Proof System

The proof system uses:
- **Poseidon hash** for commitments — ZK-circuit-friendly, fast on mobile, post-quantum secure
- **Plonky2** for the zero-knowledge proving system — no trusted setup, ~1 second proving time on modern smartphones
- **Recursive proof aggregation** for multi-session verification (future versions)

### 6.3 Privacy Properties

| Property | Guarantee |
|:---|:---|
| Raw motion data never leaves the device | ✅ On-device processing only |
| Motion Signature vector never transmitted | ✅ Only ZK proof transmitted |
| Proofs are unlinkable across sessions | ✅ Fresh salt per proof |
| Verifier learns only presence status | ✅ ZK ensures no information leakage |
| No biometric template stored anywhere | ✅ Enrollment signature is a mathematical vector, not a biometric |

### 7.2 Key Design Decisions

1. **Raw motion data never leaves the device.** All processing — pose estimation, feature extraction, signature computation, proof generation — executes locally.
2. **Verification nodes are stateless.** They store only nonces (for replay prevention, with TTL) and revocation lists. No user data, no motion history, no behavioral profiles.
3. **The protocol is platform-agnostic.** Any device with a camera, an IMU, and a TEE/secure element can participate. Device capabilities are tiered, not binary.

## 8. Proof of Intent: The First Application

### 8.1 The Wedge

The protocol's first application is **Proof of Intent** — binding a high-value operation to verified human presence.

**Scenario**: A user's AI agent holds the private key to a wallet containing $500K. The agent proposes a transfer. Before execution, the protocol verifies that the human who enrolled the agent is physically present and authorizing this specific operation.

The presence verification is cryptographically bound to the operation:

$$\text{IntentProof} = \text{Sign}_{\text{device}}(\text{PresenceScore}, \text{OperationHash}, \text{Timestamp})$$

An IntentProof is:
- Non-transferable (bound to a specific operation)
- Time-limited (valid for a single use)
- Verifiable by smart contracts, agents, and applications

### 8.2 Why Proof of Intent First

Among all possible applications, Proof of Intent is the sharpest wedge because:
- **Pain point is acute**: Losing $500K to an unauthorized agent transfer is a "hair on fire" problem
- **Willingness to pay is high**: Users will adopt friction for high-value operations
- **Ecosystem dependency is low**: Does not require network effects to be useful
- **Technical fit is natural**: Challenge-response + ZK + multi-sensor = purpose-built for this

### 8.3 Beyond Proof of Intent

The protocol roadmap extends from single-operation verification to a full Presence Layer:

| Version | Capability |
|:---|:---|
| **v1.0 — Proof of Intent** | Single high-value operation verification |
| **v1.1 — Continuous Presence** | Ongoing presence verification for games, VR, AI conversations |
| **v1.2 — Presence Layer** | Reputation, Presence Graph, consensus for DAOs and social platforms |
| **v1.3 — Presence Economy** | Presence as a verifiable scarce resource, economic incentives |

## 10. Conclusion

The internet was built on accounts. Web3 was built on keys. Both prove ownership of a credential. Neither proves the presence of a human.

As AI systems become indistinguishable from humans in every static modality, this gap becomes the most critical vulnerability in digital infrastructure. A bot can hold a private key. An AI can present a face. An agent can authorize a transaction. The question is no longer "does this credential match?" but "is a human present right now?"

MyShape answers this question with a protocol that is:
- **Active**, not passive — unpredictable challenges, not static observation
- **Multi-modal**, not single-sensor — camera + IMU, not just video
- **Economic**, not absolutist — security through engineered cost asymmetry
- **Private**, not extractive — zero-knowledge proofs, not biometric databases
- **Layered**, not monolithic — five verification factors, each an independent defense

The protocol does not claim to be perfect. It claims to be honest — about its assumptions, its limitations, and the evolving threat landscape it operates within. Its security is grounded in the irreducible physics of the human body and the economics of adversarial cost.

In an era where AI can forge everything static, the last sovereign signal is motion — real-time, high-entropy, biologically grounded, and provably present.
